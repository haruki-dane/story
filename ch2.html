Chapter 2 :The Lily in the Graveyard

She came to his circus expecting nothing. Then his gaze found hers, and something in his cold machinery 'stuttered'.

She found him afterward, unpainted in the moonlight.

"Thou gazed upon me," he whispered, "as if thou couldst see the phantom beneath the paint."

"I merely felt the ache of thee," she replied. "It resonated with mine own."

He told her of the others. The merchant's daughter. The baroness's ward. The art of the almost-touch.

"I know," she said. "I watched thee work. Thou art not the first monster I have loved. Thou art simply the first who 'admitted' it."

And Jax, who had seduced without feeling for twenty years, felt the terrifying unfamiliar sensation of being 'seen'.

They fell in love in the dark behind the tent. She loved the hollow boy beneath the monster. He loved the way she looked upon the worst of him and did not flinch.

But Jax faced a choice:Abandon revenge for her, or use her to complete it.

But the machinery of vengeance was already in motion.His choice was still destruction and the result would be blood.
