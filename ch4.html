Chapter 3 :The Space She Left Behind

They fled together into the dark. For twenty years, they knew a stolen, impossible happiness—not pure, but 'real'. Jax learned to touch without strategy. Luna taught him that a real touch, even one that trembles, is worth a thousand almosts.

Then she died. He held her hand at the end. Palm to palm. He felt her pulse stop.

And Jax faced his final choice:Shatter into the monster again, or honor her by becoming the man she saw in him.

And Jax did not shatter. She had built something in him—a foundation.

He returned to the road. A small tent. Thin audiences. Every night, he raised his hand toward the empty space beside him. A breath from nothing.

'The almost-touch.'

But now it was not a weapon. It was a 'prayer'.

"I see thee," he whispered. "In every shadow. In every hollow space. I see thee, and I choose thee."

And sometimes—in the silence, in the dark—he heard her voice, soft as wind:

"I see thee too."
