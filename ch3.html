Chapter 3 :The Horror and the Recognition

The final night came. In the front row sat the Montgomerys—and beside them, Luna, smiling with trust he was about to shatter.

"Dost thou recall," Jax murmured, "a humble caravan… upon a night of weeping skies?"

Then he laughed—a jagged, hollow sound like shattered glass. He moved with cold, swift grace. There was no struggle. Just the final settling of a ledger written in blood.

Luna stood frozen. The horror in her eyes was absolute.

But she did not scream. She took his hands and pressed them against her own face. Skin to skin. Palm to cheek. The touch he had never given anyone.

"I hated them," she said. "I dreamed of their dying. Thou art not the first monster I have loved. Thou art simply the first who 'admitted' it."

Jax fell to his knees and wept—not the performer's tear, but the ugly weeping of a child.

For the first time, he faced a true choice:Continue as the hollow monster, or become something new.

"I know not how to do this," he said.

"Then learn," she replied. "With me. Only with me. When the tent is empty, I want the hand that 'touches'. Not the almost. Not the door that opens to nothing."

He chose her. That was the result.

She kissed him—tasting of blood and tears and the salt of two hollow souls who had found, in each other's emptiness, a shape that fit.
