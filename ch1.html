Chapter 1 :The Hollow Man

The circus did not entertain. It devoured. At its heart stood Jax-a clown whose motto was whispered like a prayer: "I grant thee not mere laughter....I restore that which the cruel world hath sought to plunder from thy weary soul."He was perfection incarnate. He was not just a clown but a minor reflecting the secret,seducing with 'understanding'- he made each woman feel special.

To the world,he was kind, gentle but that man was naught but a hollow husk dressed in painted seduction. Within Jax dwelt a silence so profound it swallowed the every light of the moon and made the stars feel like apologies. Once,there had been warmth-the gentle cadence of his parent;s voices threading through the rustic joy of a traveling caravan, the lullayby of wheels on dirt roads. Then, the night arrived-a tempest of rain and shadows that smelled of copper and ruination. Two figures emerged from the gloom, and earth drank deep of a blood that would forever stain the tapestry of his memory, soaking into the soil until the ground itself remembered.

The boy died. What remained was a vessel of cold vengance wrapped in a devasting beauty. He made a choice that night:"I will never love again. I will only take." This was the first result of a choice-a hollow man who could seduce but never feel.

The boy perished in that downpour, his innocence torn from him like a veil. The monster was born in the aftermath-naked,shivering, and hungry.Jax didnot hasten his vengeance. He curated it. He became a master he called the almost-touch-the hands held a breath from a woman's skin,the warmth never offered, the door opened to an empty room.He shred his humanity piece by piece-first his laughter, then his mercy, then his capacity for anything resembling tenderness.There were others, dozens-a constellation of ruined young women scattered across the countryside like a fallen petals, each one carrying the memory of a glance that promised everything and delieverd nothing. What remained was a vessel of cold vegenance wrapped in devasting beauty.

He found them at last. The Montgomerys, the architects of his ruin. And they had a daughter "Luna".
